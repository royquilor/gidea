2013

During the first months of 2013 there have been major improvements to the church. New windows have been installed in the roof and all the ground level windows and doors have been replaced. There is a new confessional which allows people to choose between kneeling to receive the sacrament or sitting face-to-face with the priest. There is also a disabled toilet.

On 3rd July 2013 the parish celebrated these developments with a special Service of Blessing and Thanksgiving. We were happy to welcome clergy who are friends of the parish, including Fr Francis Van Son, who helps out regularly in the parish, the local dean Fr Tom Jordan, and Fr Roderick Hindley from the local Anglican parish of St Alban’s.

We are grateful to Anne Dixon and Charlene Campbell of Green Tea Architects, who led the project, to Howard Denmark, our builders, and to A2Z, who designed and installed the ground floor windows and doors. Our parishioner, Mr Bob Hind, was project manager.

The Chi Rho Symbol

Visitors to the church are struck by the Chi-Rho etching on all the ground-floor doors and windows. These are two Greek letters which are superimposed in this very ancient symbol for Christ: Chi, which resembles a Latin X, and Rho, which is like a Latin P. They are the first two letters of Christos, the Greek word meaning ‘Christ’.

As you approach the Church the symbol welcomes you. Once inside the Church the symbol is legible from the inside. The multiple windows and doors constantly remind us that Christ lies at the heart of our faith, and that our parish is uniquely dedicated to him. We are ‘Christians’, followers of Christ.

Stage 2

The next stage, stage 2 of the refurbishment plan, will address the liturgical issues concerning the Sanctuary, and redecorate the inside of the church. Outline plans to slightly alter the floor plan of the Sanctuary have been drawn up. This will enable the provision of a Baptistery and provide space for a new altar, ambo, presidential chair and Altar Server seating. We are complying with the instructions given to us by Bishop Thomas and Bishop Alan in this regard. The plans need to follow the instructions and guidance covering these matters. Discussions with our architect have also included the decoration of the walls around and ceiling above the Sanctuary.

My guestimate for the work is about £90,000 and you have to date donated and raised in excess of £65,000, for which we are deeply grateful. Your donations to the Refurbishment Fund are still vitally important. The work that is to be carried out will provide a worthy place of worship for at least t the next 30 to 50 years. We are a growing and vibrant parish and it is our duty to provide for ourselves and the next generation of Gidea Park Parishioners.

Future refurbishment and development, Stages 3 and 4.

Stage 3
Private prayer chapel - Cry Chapel

Here is a proposal for you to consider and tell me your thoughts. Many visitors and a number of Gidea Park parishioners ask why the church is only unlocked for Masses and Services as they would like to have the opportunity to pop in for a few moments of quiet prayer at other times. The answer is “a need for security”.

However, imagine the area on the right of the Narthex that includes the flower storage cupboard and the area that, until lately, housed the statue of Our Lady being converted into a small chapel with a glass wall looking into the main church.

The inner main church doors could be locked, providing security, with the outside doors being unlocked and so giving access to the small chapel. The chapel could be dual purpose furnished to enable its use as a quiet prayer chapel out of Mass/Service times and an area, often referred to as a “Cry Chapel” where adults could take young children during Mass/Services.

Please let Bob know what you think of the idea.

Stage 4
Extension to the church

At the Parish meeting held in the Church Hall early last year, I presented an outline for an extension to the rear of the church to mirror the parts of the church that constitute the Sacristy and boiler house.

Fr. Adrian and I have had discussions with our architect and the Diocesan Building Officer to explore the feasibility of this and it would seem that there are no physical problems to prevent this construction.

The advantages to the church would be the provision of a Lady Chapel that would be open to the main church, provision of extra room for the Baptistery, if this is to be on the left hand side of the Sanctuary, an extra seating area for the church and a flower arranging workroom and storage facility to replace the storage area lost if the “Cry Chapel” idea is implemented Fr. Adrian and I would be pleased to hear your comments on these works.

Finance implications

The Diocese demands that any major capital expenditure by a parish must be self- funding. If the expenditure is from reserves, then plans must be put in place to replenish the money in a reasonable time period. If the money is not in place then detailed plans of how the money is to be raised must be provided.

The new benches have been paid for from Parish reserve funds as they were classed as necessary replacements as the old benches were declared a Health and Safety risk, evidenced by two injury claims.

Our Church Refurbishment Fund currently stands at just over £65,000. Many thanks for your generosity so far. The rough estimates for Stage 2 are about £90,000 so we are just over two thirds of the way to what we need.

Please, please, please support our refurbishment fund-raising activities and donate as generously as possible to the Refurbishment Fund collections.
